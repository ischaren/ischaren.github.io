Design, Expression, and Socio-Enviromental Considerations


Design is the construction of human experience. It is a way of thinking and a method for problem solving.
Art is an expression of human experience. It is a language of culture that enables communication of ideas beyond words.
Engineering is technical logistics of solution execution. It is the means by which solutions gain physicality.
Engineering and art are distinct from design, yet integral to its successful execution.
Human experience is relationship with ourselves, the natural world, and the built environment.
It is necessary to consider environmental, sociological, and precedent factors in the construction of human experience.
An integrative study of design, therefore, can justifiably span the disciplines of Studio Art, Engineering, Environmental Studies and Sociology in addition to applications of design thinking.
This is the basis for my integrative major, “Design, Build and Socio-Environmental Considerations.”

Guiding Questions


How can advancements in technology serve the planet and society?
What are the implications of the increasing presence of the built environment over the natural environment?
How can cooperative interdisciplinary work be effective?
How is design thinking influenced by moral and ethical questions?
What are my actual guiding questions?
